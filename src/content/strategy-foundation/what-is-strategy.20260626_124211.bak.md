# What Is Strategy? It's a Lot Simpler Than You Think

This document explores the concept of **strategy** as defined by Felix Oberholzer-Gee, emphasizing its simplicity and core components. Understanding strategy is crucial for organizations aiming to create value for customers, employees, and suppliers. This structured overview will clarify the definition of strategy, its components, and practical applications.

## Definition of Strategy

**Strategy** is a plan to create value. It involves how a company intends to generate value for its stakeholders, including customers, employees, and suppliers. While financial metrics like profitability and return on invested capital are important, they are outcomes of a well-defined strategy rather than its starting point.

## The Value Stick Framework

### Definition
The **Value Stick** is a visual representation that illustrates the relationship between **willingness to pay** and **willingness to sell**. 

### Key Questions
- What is the difference between willingness to pay and willingness to sell?
- How can a company increase willingness to pay?
- What strategies can reduce willingness to sell?

### Examples
- **Willingness to Pay**: A customer may be willing to pay $8 for a cup of coffee but buys it for $2 at Dunkin' Donuts, creating significant value.
- **Willingness to Sell**: An employee's willingness to sell their labor is influenced by job satisfaction, compensation, and working conditions.

### How to Apply
To leverage the Value Stick:
1. Assess customer willingness to pay through market research.
2. Evaluate employee willingness to sell by conducting engagement surveys.
3. Identify gaps between these measures to create strategies that enhance value.

## Increasing Willingness to Pay

### Definition
Willingness to pay refers to the maximum price a customer is willing to pay for a product or service.

### Key Strategies
1. **Improve Product Quality**: Higher quality increases customer appeal.
2. **Utilize Complements**: Products that enhance the primary offering can boost willingness to pay (e.g., printers and cartridges).
3. **Leverage Network Effects**: Popular products can increase customer demand (e.g., social media platforms).

### Examples
- **Complements**: The relationship between razors and razor blades.
- **Network Effects**: The increased value of social media platforms as more users join.

### How to Apply
- Invest in product development to enhance quality.
- Explore partnerships that create complementary products.
- Foster community engagement to enhance network effects.

## Decreasing Willingness to Sell

### Definition
Willingness to sell is the minimum compensation an employee would accept to work for a company.

### Key Strategies
1. **Increase Compensation**: Higher salaries can attract talent but may not create new value.
2. **Enhance Job Quality**: Improving working conditions and job satisfaction can lower willingness to sell, creating value.

### Examples
- **Best Buy's Strategy**: By transforming stores into "stores within stores," Best Buy improved employee engagement and job satisfaction, reducing willingness to sell.

### How to Apply
- Conduct employee satisfaction surveys to identify areas for improvement.
- Implement policies that enhance job conditions, such as flexible working arrangements and professional development opportunities.

## Case Study: Best Buy

### Background
Best Buy faced significant challenges a decade ago, including competition from online retailers like Amazon and substantial financial losses.

### Strategy Implementation
1. **Reimagining Stores**: Best Buy began using its stores as distribution centers, improving shipping times and customer service.
2. **Partnerships with Brands**: Collaborated with companies like Microsoft and Samsung to create "store within a store" experiences, enhancing customer engagement and reducing costs.

### Results
- Increased customer willingness to pay due to better service and product availability.
- Reduced employee willingness to sell through improved job satisfaction.
- Achieved a return on invested capital exceeding 20%, reversing previous losses.

## Key Takeaways
- Strategy is fundamentally about creating value, not just financial metrics.
- The Value Stick framework helps visualize the relationship between customer and employee value.
- Increasing willingness to pay can be achieved through quality, complements, and network effects.
- Decreasing willingness to sell involves enhancing job quality rather than just increasing compensation.
- Best Buy's strategic transformation illustrates the practical application of these concepts, leading to significant business recovery and growth.

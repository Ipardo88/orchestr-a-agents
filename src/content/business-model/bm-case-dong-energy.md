# Dong Energy: Business Model Reinvention While Profitable

This document explores the strategic business model reinvention of Dong Energy, highlighting the balance between improving existing business models and inventing new ones. Understanding this balance is crucial for organizations aiming to sustain growth and competitiveness in changing markets.

## Improving vs. Inventing Business Models

### Definition
- **Improving Business Models**: Involves optimizing existing operations, reducing costs, and extending product lines to maximize revenue from current business models before they mature or decline.
- **Inventing Business Models**: Focuses on designing new business models and investing in growth engines that address critical customer problems, effectively creating new revenue streams.

### Key Questions
1. How can organizations optimize their current business models?
2. When should companies begin to invest in new business models?
3. What external factors should be considered when transitioning from one business model to another?

### Examples
- **Dong Energy**: Transitioned from a reliance on fossil fuels to becoming a leader in offshore wind energy, recognizing the need for change before facing declining revenues.

### How to Apply
- Organizations should continuously assess their business models for optimization opportunities while simultaneously exploring new growth avenues, especially when current models are performing well.

## Dong Energy's Strategic Shift

### Background
Dong Energy, now known as Ørsted, was primarily focused on oil, gas, and coal energy. However, recognizing external pressures such as:
- Stricter CO2 emission regulations in Europe
- Negative public sentiment towards fossil fuels
- The decreasing cost of renewable energy
- Government incentives for cleaner energy

Dong Energy made a strategic decision to reinvent itself.

### Key Actions
1. **Reinvestment Strategy**: In 2015, 75% of Dong's capital was reinvested in wind energy, while only 18% of revenues came from oil and gas.
2. **Leveraging Expertise**: The company utilized its existing knowledge in offshore oil and gas to develop offshore wind farms.
3. **Financing New Ventures**: Dong used cash flow from its traditional business to fund the transition to renewable energy.

### Challenges Faced
- Dependency on subsidies for wind energy revenues, which are expected to diminish over time.
- A significant decline in revenues from oil and gas, creating financial pressure during the transition.
- The need to effectively lower the cost of wind energy to remain competitive against traditional utilities.

### Outcome
Despite the challenges, Dong Energy successfully executed an Initial Public Offering (IPO) in 2016, valued at $16 billion, marking it as the largest European IPO at that time. This success was attributed to the company's proactive approach to reinventing its business model while still thriving in its traditional market.

## Lessons Learned

### Key Takeaways
- **Proactive Reinvention**: Companies should begin exploring new business models while their current models are still successful to avoid crisis-driven transitions.
- **Investment in Innovation**: Allocating resources to new growth engines is essential for long-term sustainability.
- **Market Awareness**: Understanding external factors and market trends can guide strategic decisions in business model innovation.
- **Financial Strategy**: Using existing business cash flows to finance new ventures can mitigate risks during transitions.
- **Long-term Vision**: Establishing a clear vision for future growth is critical to navigating the challenges of business model reinvention.

By learning from Dong Energy's experience, organizations can better position themselves to adapt to market changes and ensure ongoing success.

# Playing with Numbers and Improving Existing Business Models

This document explores how existing business models can be improved through innovative changes, using Nike's running shoe business model as a case study. By analyzing the impact of new technologies and strategies on profitability, coaches and agents can better understand how to leverage numbers for business model innovation.

## Overview of Nike's Running Shoe Business Model

### Definition
Nike's running shoe business model involves selling shoes through distributors to global retailers, generating revenue through wholesale fees.

### Key Components
- **Revenue Stream**: Approximately **$50** per shoe sold through **52,000 retailers**, leading to an estimated **$3 billion** in revenue.
- **Cost Structure**: Includes research and development, cost of sales, selling and administrative expenses, and promotion and advertising.
- **Profit**: Estimated at **$380 million** for running shoes.

### Key Questions
- How can existing business models be optimized for better profitability?
- What role does technology play in transforming traditional manufacturing processes?

### Example: Nike's Running Shoe Model
Nike's business model for running shoes relies heavily on low-cost manufacturing in countries like **China**, **Cambodia**, and **Vietnam**. The company utilizes a combination of brand strength and athlete endorsements to enhance its market presence.

## Innovation Through Flyknit Technology

### Definition
**Flyknit technology** is a manufacturing process that uses proprietary software to instruct knitting machines to create the upper part of a shoe in one piece, significantly reducing the need for manual labor.

### Key Questions
- What are the implications of adopting Flyknit technology on cost structure?
- How does this technology affect the overall business model and profitability?

### Impact on Business Model
- **Cost Reduction**: Flyknit technology allows for a significant decrease in the cost of sales by minimizing reliance on large factories and manual labor.
- **Profit Increase**: Nike's CEO, **Mark Parker**, indicated that this innovation could lead to **3.7 times** more profit due to the reduced manufacturing costs.

### How to Apply
- Evaluate existing manufacturing processes and identify areas where technology can streamline operations.
- Consider the scalability of new technologies across different product lines to maximize profitability.

## Conclusion

Nike's strategic shift to incorporate Flyknit technology exemplifies how playing with numbers can lead to substantial improvements in existing business models. By leveraging innovative manufacturing techniques, companies can enhance their profitability and operational efficiency.

## Key Takeaways
- Existing business models can be improved through innovative technologies.
- Nike's running shoe model generates significant revenue and profit through strategic partnerships and cost management.
- Flyknit technology represents a transformative approach to manufacturing, reducing costs and increasing profit margins.
- Companies should continuously evaluate their business models for opportunities to innovate and optimize.
- Understanding the financial implications of operational changes is crucial for business success.

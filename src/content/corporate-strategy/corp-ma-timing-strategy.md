# M&A Strategy — Now Is the Time to Strike

This document covers insights from Doug Peterson, CEO of S&P Global, regarding the strategic timing of mergers and acquisitions (M&A), particularly in the context of the company's merger with IHS Markit. Understanding the factors that influence M&A decisions and the importance of cultural alignment can help organizations navigate similar strategic initiatives effectively.

## Overview of S&P Global

S&P Global is a leading provider of data and analytics that assist clients in making informed business decisions. The company operates across various divisions, including ratings, indices, market intelligence, commodity insights, and mobility. With a client base in over 150 countries, S&P Global is experiencing significant growth, particularly in emerging markets, and is witnessing a shift from bank markets to capital markets.

## Key Moments in S&P Global's Strategy

### Transition to S&P Global Brand

- **Definition**: The rebranding from McGraw-Hill Financial to S&P Global was a pivotal moment that unified the organization under a single vision.
- **Key Questions**:
  - How can a unified brand enhance strategic alignment?
  - What are the implications of rebranding on company culture and operations?
- **Examples**: The merger with IHS Markit exemplifies the strategic benefits of a cohesive brand identity.
- **How to Apply**: Organizations should assess their brand alignment with strategic goals and consider rebranding when necessary to unify vision and operations.

### Strategic Themes Driving M&A

- **Definition**: M&A strategy should be driven by client needs and market trends, including sustainability and digital transformation.
- **Key Questions**:
  - What are the emerging trends that could impact our business?
  - How can we fulfill client needs through strategic partnerships or acquisitions?
- **Examples**: S&P Global identified the need to enhance capabilities in sustainability and digital transformation, leading to the merger with IHS Markit.
- **How to Apply**: Regularly evaluate market trends and client needs to inform M&A strategies.

## The Merger with IHS Markit

### Rationale for the Merger

- **Definition**: The merger was driven by the complementary strengths of both companies, enhancing data and analytics capabilities.
- **Key Questions**:
  - What synergies exist between potential merger partners?
  - How can the merger enhance our competitive position?
- **Examples**: IHS Markit provided data analytics that S&P Global lacked, while S&P Global offered benchmarks and technology platforms.
- **How to Apply**: Conduct thorough due diligence to identify complementary strengths before pursuing a merger.

### Execution of the Merger

1. **Initial Contact**: Doug Peterson initiated discussions with IHS Markit's CEO, Lance Uggla, after board approval.
2. **Building Trust**: Personal relationships and trust facilitated open discussions.
3. **Management Meetings**: Conducted in-person meetings during COVID-19 to foster collaboration and define organizational structure.
4. **Cultural Integration**: Focused on aligning company cultures to ensure a smooth transition.

### Challenges and Opportunities

- **Opportunities**:
  - Enhanced capabilities and market position.
  - Strengthened cultural alignment between organizations.
- **Challenges**:
  - Navigating the complexities of merging two large organizations.
  - Maintaining clear communication and engagement with employees during the transition.

## Importance of Culture in M&A

- **Definition**: Organizational culture plays a critical role in the success of mergers and acquisitions.
- **Key Questions**:
  - How can we assess and align the cultures of merging organizations?
  - What strategies can we implement to foster a unified culture post-merger?
- **Examples**: S&P Global and IHS Markit shared similar people-oriented and customer-centric cultures, easing integration.
- **How to Apply**: Prioritize cultural alignment in M&A planning and execution to enhance the likelihood of success.

## Key Takeaways

- M&A can be a strategic opportunity when organizations have both **financial** and **managerial capacity**.
- A unified brand can enhance strategic alignment and operational effectiveness.
- Understanding client needs and market trends is crucial for informing M&A strategies.
- Building trust and personal relationships can facilitate smoother negotiations and integrations.
- Cultural alignment is essential for the success of mergers; organizations should prioritize this aspect during the integration process.
- Effective communication and engagement with employees are vital during significant organizational changes.

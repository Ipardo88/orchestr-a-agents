45 Alfred Rappaport, “The Affordable Dividend Approach to Equity Valuation,” Financial Analysts Journal, Vol. 
42, No. 4, July-August 1986, 52-58. The formula for the affordable dividend payout rate (payout as a percent of 
net income) is as follows: 
 
= _____________sales growth × (working capital rate + fixed capital rate)______________ 
 (1 + sales growth) × operating profit margin × (1 – tax rate) × (1 + target debt/equity ratio) 
 
The working capital rate is the amount of additional working capital that is required as a percentage of the change 
in sales. For example, if the rate is 15 percent and sales grow $100, working capital will grow by $15. The fixed 
capital rate equals the capital expenditures minus depreciation that are required as a percentage of the change 
in sales. If capital expenditures are $50, depreciation is $25, and sales grow $100, fixed capital will grow by $25. 
The fixed capital rate is 25 percent.
